# American-express-credit-risk-dashboard

# PROJECT OVERVIEW
## 📌 Project Overview

The **American Express Credit Risk Dashboard** is an end-to-end data analytics project developed using **SQL Server, Power BI, Power Query, DAX, and Excel**. The project focuses on analyzing customer financial and credit data to identify key factors associated with credit default risk.

The analysis begins with data cleaning and transformation in SQL and Power Query, followed by customer segmentation and business-driven analysis. Interactive Power BI dashboards were then created to provide insights into customer demographics, credit behavior, financial health, and default patterns.

The dashboard is divided into two pages:
* **Customer Portfolio Overview:** Presents key customer metrics, income distribution, credit score analysis, asset ownership, and customer segmentation.
* **Credit Risk & Default Analysis:** Identifies high-risk customer segments by analyzing default rates across income groups, occupations, credit score bands, previous default history, and credit utilization.

The project demonstrates how data can be transformed into actionable business insights that support better credit risk assessment, portfolio monitoring, and informed lending decisions. It showcases practical skills in **SQL querying, data cleaning, DAX calculations, data modeling, and interactive dashboard development**, making it a real-world business intelligence project inspired by the financial services industry.

# BUSINESS PROBLEM

Financial institutions issue credit cards and loans to millions of customers every year. One of their biggest challenges is identifying customers who are likely to default on their repayments while continuing to provide credit to reliable customers.

Poor credit risk assessment can lead to increased financial losses, higher default rates, and inefficient lending decisions. Therefore, organizations need data-driven insights to evaluate customer risk based on financial behavior, credit history, income, and other relevant factors.

This project addresses this challenge by analyzing customer credit data to identify the key drivers of default risk. Using SQL and Power BI, the dashboard helps answer important business questions such as:

* Which customer segments have the highest default rates?
* How does default risk vary across income groups and credit score bands?
* Does previous default history increase the likelihood of future defaults?
* How does credit utilization influence default risk?
* Which customers require closer monitoring based on their overall risk profile?

The insights generated from this dashboard enable financial institutions to strengthen credit risk management, improve lending decisions, optimize customer monitoring, and reduce potential credit losses.

## 📊 Dashboard – Page 1: Customer Portfolio Overview

The **Customer Portfolio Overview** dashboard provides a comprehensive summary of the customer base by analyzing demographic, financial, and credit-related attributes. It helps stakeholders understand the overall customer portfolio before performing detailed risk analysis.

### Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

* Total Customers
* Average Annual Income
* Average Credit Score
* Average Credit Limit

### Visualizations

* **Average Credit Limit by Income Group** – Compares the average credit limit across different income segments.
* **Customer Distribution by Credit Score Band** – Shows the proportion of customers in Poor, Fair, Good, and Excellent credit score categories.
* **Customer Distribution by Occupation** – Identifies the largest customer occupation groups.
* **Asset Ownership Profile** – Analyzes customers based on house and car ownership.
* **Previous Default Status** – Displays the percentage of customers with and without previous defaults.

### Business Value

This dashboard provides an overall understanding of the customer portfolio by highlighting customer demographics, financial capacity, creditworthiness, and ownership patterns. These insights help financial institutions identify customer segments, understand portfolio composition, and establish a strong foundation for further credit risk analysis.
